## Decisions made

1. **iPhone 12 (not 15/16 Pro) as canonical device** - per Section 9 of AI-UGC-PROMPT-EXAMPLES.md.
   Older device = grainier output, less HDR polish. Anchor in `ugc-realism.ts` const.

2. **Per-half variation, not locked-same** - real customer testimonials (OsloSkinLab examples)
   show different angles/distances/expressions between halves. They're two separate selfies on
   different days, not a coordinated shoot. Locked-same was a misread of William's earlier
   instruction.

3. **Cover-fit by default, zoom < 1 enables letterbox** - when ratio changes from source to e.g.
   1:1, cover-fit fills with cropping. User can zoom out below 1 to reveal more of source half,
   gradually transitioning to contain-fit with white letterbox.

4. **No "enable" toggle for crop/zoom controls** - default zoom=1/pan=0 is already no-op, the
   toggle was just bonus clicks. William explicitly called this out.

5. **Browse-and-edit > in-tool picker** - the standalone tool's "Pick from assets" mode is now a
   fallback. Primary flow: browse Assets normally with full-aspect thumbnails, click image to
   preview, "Edit (Post Production)" button takes you to the editor with the asset preselected.
   Asset previews now support arrow-key navigation.

6. **Drop the Product picker / filter** - the workspace already implies product (Hydro13 workspace
   = Hydro13 assets). Having an additional product dropdown was redundant from pre-workspace days.

7. **Final-encode quality boost when overlay present** - degradation jpegQuality of 20-66 was
   smashing the freshly-drawn day labels / arrow / divider. Now applyPipeline bumps final encode
   to >= 92 whenever any overlay is enabled. Underlying image artifacts already baked into pixel
   data by the multi-pass loop, so high final encode preserves both.

8. **localStorage for user presets** - simpler than DB persistence for now. Two keys:
   `content-hub-degradation-presets-v1` + `content-hub-overlay-presets-v1`. Forward-compat via
   default-merge on load.
