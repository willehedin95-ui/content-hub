## Our Unique Position

We are NOT a pure affiliate site. We are an ecommerce brand (HappySleep pillows, Hydro13 hydrogen water) that also earns affiliate commission on competitor products via Awin.

**Why this is better than pure affiliate:**
- First-hand product expertise (genuine E-E-A-T)
- Real customer data and testimonials
- Highest margin on our own product sales
- Google is actively favoring brands over affiliates in search results (Amsive 2024 study)
- PLUS we still earn affiliate commission (Awin) on competitor links
- Three markets (SE/NO/DK) × multiple categories = compounding affiliate revenue

**The play:** Build "bäst i test" pages where our product wins #1 (genuine recommendation), competitors get honest reviews with Awin affiliate links. Earn from own sales + affiliate commissions.

**Awin account:** Incensor AB (1949105), SEK programmes. As of 2026-03-23: 1,357 SEK approved, 186 SEK pending review.

**Key constraint:** Reviews of competitors must be genuinely honest and detailed. Not "our pillow: 10/10, everything else: 5/10." Google (and readers) can spot self-serving bias. The Swedish sites we analyzed all give competitors solid ratings — that builds trust.

## The Universal "Best X" Template

This is the exact structure every successful affiliate site uses — Wirecutter, kuddguiden, kostnörd, testkompassen, NerdWallet. Works for any product category.

```
H1: "Bästa [produkt] 2026 — Testad & rankad"
├── Quick verdict box (top 3 picks with "Best for..." labels)
├── Comparison table (product, rating, price, key spec, buy link)
├── Individual product reviews (5-7 products)
│   ├── Rating (X/10)
│   ├── Image (MUST be original, not stock)
│   ├── 300-500 word review
│   ├── Pros list
│   ├── Cons list
│   ├── Specs table
│   └── CTA button (affiliate link or own product link)
├── How we tested (methodology — critical for E-E-A-T)
├── Buyer's guide (what to look for)
├── FAQ (6-10 questions, targets featured snippets)
└── Sources / references
```

**Word count:** 3,000-5,000 for solid pages. Swedish competitors go 8,000-13,000+.

**CTA placement:** Exactly 3 — after quick verdict, middle of reviews, after final verdict.

**Schema markup:**
- `FAQPage` (for FAQ rich snippets in SERPs)
- `Article` (with named author + credentials)
- `BreadcrumbList` (navigation hierarchy)
- `Product` + `Review` optional but helpful

## Swedish Competitors Analyzed

### Kuddguiden.se (Ranks #1 for "bäst i test kudde")

- **Model:** Exact-match domain ("The Pillow Guide"), niche affiliate
- **Strategy:** Homepage IS the money page — all domain authority flows to primary keyword
- **Content:** ~4,500 words, 6 products, comparison table, buyer's guide, 9 FAQs
- **Scale:** Only ~15 pages total — extremely focused niche site
- **Testing:** No original testing (aggregates verified customer reviews from Swedish retailers)
- **Template:** Every "bäst i test" page follows identical structure
- **Strengths:** Tight topical authority, FAQPage schema, year-updated content, clean comparison table

### Kostnörd.se (Ranks #2)

- **Model:** Multi-vertical affiliate (supplements + fitness + sleep)
- **Scale:** 250+ pages across two verticals, ~170 supplement + ~80 training/health
- **Content:** ~11,000 words per review page, always exactly 7 products
- **Rating system:** X.X/5 with 5 sub-criteria rating bars per product
- **Testing claims:** 9 weeks, 5 testers, 16 pillows — detailed methodology section
- **Authors:** Named editors with credentials (nutritionist, personal trainer)
- **Strengths:** Content depth, E-E-A-T signals, rigid scalable template

### Testkompassen.se (Ranks #3)

- **Model:** Massive multi-category affiliate (20 categories)
- **Scale:** Hundreds of review pages, 17 pages in bedroom subcategory alone
- **Content:** ~8,000-10,000 words per page, always exactly 5 products
- **Rating system:** X.X/10 with weighted criteria (e.g. ergonomic support 25%, material 15%)
- **Testing:** Explicitly admits NO physical testing — aggregates external data
- **Template:** Extremely rigid, suggests semi-automated production
- **Strengths:** Massive category breadth, detailed weighted scoring

### Key Observations

All three follow the SAME template. Differences are:
- **Depth:** 4,500 vs 11,000 vs 8,000 words
- **Breadth:** 15 pages vs 250+ vs hundreds
- **Credibility:** Named testers vs generic "our team"
- **Testing claims:** Varies from "we analyze reviews" to "we tested for 9 weeks"

None have real testing labs. All are vulnerable to Google's Helpful Content Update.

## The Swedish "Bäst i Test" Ecosystem

### Hierarchy (most to least credible)

```
TRUST LEVEL (high → low)
============================================
Råd & Rön (radron.se)     — Lab testing since 1958, now owned by Bonnier/DN
Testfakta (testfakta.se)   — 40+ accredited labs, 79% brand recognition
                             (BUT: companies pay for tests and choose whether to publish)
test.se                    — Market leader, ~290K visits/mo. Pure aggregator, zero testing.
best-i-test.nu             — Real testing, named reviewers, 15-year track record
bäst-i-testet.se           — Hands-on testing, 3 named editors
bäst-i-test.se             — Aggregator, 3 employees, cross-Scandinavian (SE/NO/DK/FI/ES)
testproffs.se              — Cross-Scandinavian network with local teams
testin.se                  — Claims 4000+ products, questionable depth
Niche sites (sovsmart, kuddguiden) — Deep expertise, narrow focus
testhuset.nu, etc.         — Low transparency, AI-assisted
============================================
```

### Price Comparison Platforms (Different Purpose)

- **Prisjakt.nu** — #1 in Sweden, CPC/CPA model, user reviews + forums
- **PriceRunner.se** — #2, owned by Klarna since 2022
- **TestFreaks** — B2B review aggregation API used by retailers (invisible infrastructure)

These tell users WHERE to buy cheapest. "Bäst i test" sites tell users WHAT to buy. The two ecosystems feed each other.

### Norwegian Market ("Best i test")

**Wide open.** No equivalent to Råd & Rön. Mostly Swedish companies expanding there.

| Site | Model |
|------|-------|
| best-i-test.nu | Swedish-founded, expanded to NO. Strongest player. |
| test.no | Aggregator, heavily ad-monetized |
| best-i-testen.no | Aggregator |
| testekspert.com | Norwegian arm of Testproffs network |
| Elkjøp "Best i test" | Retailer content marketing (not independent) |

### Danish Market ("Bedst i test")

**Stronger institutional presence** but also more regulatory enforcement.

- **Forbrugerrådet Tænk (taenk.dk)** — Denmark's Consumer Council, 306 active tests, lab-based. Closest to Consumer Reports in Scandinavia.
- **Danish Consumer Ombudsman** police-reported Obsidian Media's fake review sites (forbruger-test.dk, forbrugsguiden.dk, bedste10.dk) for concealed advertising. This hasn't happened in Sweden yet.

### Cultural Context

"Bäst i test" is deeply embedded in Swedish culture:
- 68+ year tradition of state-backed consumer testing (Råd & Rön since 1958)
- "Lagom" culture — Swedes want the "right" product, not flashiest/cheapest
- High social trust — people trust that testing is done fairly
- The phrase is so embedded it became the name of Sweden's Taskmaster TV show
- Norwegian equivalent: "Best i test", Danish: "Bedst i test"

## DTC Brands That Win at SEO

These prove a BRAND (not just an affiliate) can dominate organic search:

### Purple (Mattresses)
- **84% organic traffic growth** from just 11 interlinked educational pages about mattress types
- Most pages are NOT about their own product — they educate about categories
- Small cluster, massive results
- **Key insight:** You don't need volume. Tight clustering of 10-15 high-value pages beats a 140-post blog.

### Casper (Mattresses)
- Blog drives **40% of organic traffic**
- Mattress size guide alone ranks **top 3 for 8,000 keywords** and drives 31% of organic traffic
- Three publications: Pillow Talk (blog), Van Winkle's (standalone sleep mag), Woolly (lifestyle)

### Sleep.me (Cooling Products)
- **2,959% traffic growth in 12 months**
- From 630 to 19,274 monthly visits
- From 214 to 24,100 keywords ranked
- **How:** Built content around customer PROBLEMS (menopause + sleep, night sweats, partner sleep), not products

- Deleted low-quality content ("all killer, no filler")
- **Most relevant model for HappySleep**

### TheNap (Danish Pillow Brand)
- 250,000+ customers, 2,500+ five-star Trustpilot reviews
- Won "bedst i test" across multiple Danish review sites
- **Strategy:** Win the reviews by having a great product → affiliate sites recommend you → organic traffic flows to you

## The Affiliate SEO Playbook

### Keyword Research Process

1. **Seed list:** Product category + modifiers ("best," "review," "vs," "under X kr")
2. **Filter:** KD (keyword difficulty) ≤ 30 for new sites, ≤ 50 for established
3. **Search volume:** Sweet spot 100-3,000 monthly searches
4. **Competitor gap:** What do competitors rank for that you don't?
5. **Prioritize:** High relevance + low difficulty + commercial intent = first pages to build

### Content Architecture (Hub-and-Spoke)

```
                    [PILLAR PAGE]
                   /   |    |    \
                  /    |    |     \
          [Cluster] [Cluster] [Cluster] [Cluster]
              |         |         |
          [Cluster] [Cluster] [Cluster]
```

- **Pillar:** 3,000-5,000 words, broadest keyword (e.g., "bästa kudden")
- **Clusters:** 1,500-2,500 words each, specific long-tails
- **10-20 cluster pages per pillar** (optimal range)
- **25+ articles per cluster** for measurable ranking impact
- **Internal linking:** Every cluster links back to pillar, pillar links to every cluster, clusters cross-link to related clusters

### Content Velocity

- **Solo operator:** 8-15 articles/month (recommended)
- **Consistency > bursts:** Google needs 6-12 months of consistent publishing to recognize authority
- **Update cadence:** Quarterly minimum. AI platforms cite content 25.7% fresher than traditional search.

### The Proven Content Ratio

From Matt Diggity's study of 1,517 winning affiliate sites:

| Site Type | Informational | Commercial |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| General niche | 63% | 33% |
| Authority sites | 70% | 27% |
| YMYL (health) | 75% | 21% |

## Google's Requirements (E-E-A-T)

**71% of affiliate sites were negatively impacted by Google's December 2025 core update.**

### Experience (the critical new "E")
- Original photos/videos showing actual product use
- Testing methodology documentation
- "We tested X products over Y weeks" claims backed by evidence
- Customer testimonials with specific details
- AI can mimic Expertise and Authority but CANNOT mimic genuine Experience

### Expertise
- Named authors with verifiable credentials
- Topic-specific authors (not generalists writing about everything)
- Author pages with bio, credentials, published work

### Authoritativeness
- Comprehensive topical coverage (content clusters)
- Citations from other publications
- Long publication history on topic

### Trustworthiness
- Clear affiliate disclosure on every page (Swedish law + Google guidelines)
- Products purchased/used independently
- Honest pros AND cons (not just positives)
- Transparent testing methodology

### What Gets Penalized
- Thin affiliate listicles without real testing/insight
- Stock images (signal zero experience)
- Manufacturer copy paraphrased
- Mass-produced low-value content (AI or human)
- Missing affiliate disclosure

### The Danish Warning

Obsidian Media's fake review sites were police-reported by the Danish Consumer Ombudsman for presenting concealed advertising as independent reviews. Regulatory enforcement is coming to Sweden too. Always be transparent about: (1) you sell the #1 product, (2) competitor links are affiliate links.

## AI Content and SEO

### Google's Stance (2026)
- Google does NOT penalize AI content per se
- They penalize low-quality content regardless of production method
- 19% of top-ranking content is now AI-generated

### The Problem for Review Content
AI cannot possess "Experience" — it cannot test products, take original photos, or provide first-hand assessments. For "best X" pages specifically, pure AI fails.

### The Winning Workflow
1. **Research:** Surfer SEO / Clearscope generates content brief (NLP terms, headings, word count)
2. **AI Draft:** Claude/Jasper generates initial structure
3. **Human Expert Edit:** Add first-hand experience, original photos, testing data, personal opinions (CRITICAL step)
4. **Optimization:** Run through Surfer/Clearscope, hit 80+ content score
5. **Fact-check:** Verify specs, prices, availability
6. **Publish:** Space publications. Don't publish 50 AI articles in one week.

### For Content Hub Specifically
Our AI can generate the structure, ad copy, and informational content. But the product reviews need real experience signals — photos from our own pillow testing, customer survey data, specific observations. The "how we tested" methodology page is non-negotiable.
