## Content Types That Work (ranked)

1. **"Bäst i test" listicles** — THE dominant format in Scandinavia. Every top site uses it.
2. **Buying guides** — "Hur väljer du rätt kudde?" position-based recommendations
3. **Problem-solution articles** — "Nacksmärta på natten" style, 1500-2500 words
4. **Product comparisons** — "[A] vs [B]" head-to-head format
5. **Science/research articles** — Important for YMYL/E-E-A-T trust (critical for collagen claims)
6. **How-to/care guides** — Low competition, builds supporting authority

## Strategic Notes

- **YMYL**: Sleep and health/beauty content needs cautious, evidence-based language, study citations, and links to official sources (1177.se, Helsenorge.no, Sundhedsstyrelsen.dk)
- **EFSA compliance**: Collagen articles must use "studies suggest", "users report", "designed to support" — no direct health claims. Cite peer-reviewed studies (Proksch et al. 2014, Hexsel et al. 2017).
- **Topical authority**: Need 15-20+ interlinked articles per blog to compete. A handful won't work.
- **Pillar + cluster**: Build hub pages (comprehensive guides) with 5-8 supporting articles each
- **Update cadence**: "Bäst i test" articles need yearly title updates + quarterly content refreshes
- **Publishing target**: Launch with 10-15 foundational articles, then 2-4/month per blog
- **FAQ schema**: Add FAQ structured data to all articles
- **Competitor comparison angle**: Most collagen content is brand-owned and biased. Position Hälsobladet as the independent tester that buys everything with their own money and gives honest reviews.
