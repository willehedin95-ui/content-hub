# Question Library

Concrete question archetypes. When adapting an imported quiz, preserve the archetype while swapping the content.

---

## Entry questions (Q1-Q2, always easy)

### Age

- **Purpose**: zero cognitive load, starts the yes-chain, enables personalization story
- **Format**: single-select, 4-5 age buckets (18-24 / 25-34 / 35-44 / 45-54 / 55-64 / 65+)
- **Adaptation**: the buckets can stay identical across markets, just swap labels. Consider adding face images per bucket (model photos) — big CVR lift on beauty/health products.

### Gender

- **Purpose**: same as age, segments into parallel funnels
- **Format**: single-select (Woman / Man / Other / Prefer not to say)
- **Adaptation**: for female-targeted products, add a stronger feminine aesthetic from this question onward.

### Implicit "who is this for" (when product is gift-capable)

- **Purpose**: splits into two funnels (self-buy vs gift)
- **Example**: "Is this for yourself or a gift?"
- **When**: Welcome Baby, personalised items, couples-gift products.

## Aspirational (positive) questions

### Goal / Desired outcome

- **Purpose**: tells the quiz what to mirror on results page
- **Format**: multi-select (let them stack reasons to buy)
- **Sample phrasings**:
  - "What would you most like to improve?"
  - "Which best describes how you want to feel most days?"
  - "My dream outcome is..."
- **Adaptation**: options should align exactly with benefits your product delivers. This is where product-level research matters most.

### Motivation anchor

- **Purpose**: identity priming ("I am motivated")
- **Format**: single-select
- **Sample**: "How motivated are you to invest time and resources into finally solving this?"
- **Note the word**: "INVEST," not "spend." Frames the later offer as an investment decision.

---

## Diagnostic (neutral) questions

### Symptom multi-select (the conversion weapon)

- **Purpose**: micro-confessions. Every box checked = deeper purchase intent.
- **Format**: multi-select, 4-6 specific symptoms + one "none of the above" escape (do NOT omit this — it's a legitimacy signal).
- **Sample**: "Do any of the below apply to you? Select all that apply." then list specific, concrete symptoms.
- **Rule**: symptoms must be specific enough that people read them and go "oh shit, that's me." Not "tired" — "tired but wired at night, wake around 2-4am and struggle to fall back asleep."

### Severity / severity slider

- **Purpose**: quantify the problem. Vague → specific.
- **Format**: 5-point scale ("Nope" → "Help! It's serious") or multi-select
- **Sample**: "On a scale of 1 to 5, how much does [X] affect your daily life?"
- **Psychology**: forces the user to PHYSICALLY engage (slide/tap) with the rating, compelling them to quantify their pain.

### Duration

- **Purpose**: age-of-problem → sunk cost → more urgency to fix
- **Sample**: "How long have you been dealing with this?"
- **Options**: "Less than 3 months / 3-12 months / 1-3 years / More than 3 years"

### Current behavior / habits

- **Purpose**: neutral observation, sets up the objection handler
- **Sample**: "How often do you currently [routine]?"
- **Follow-up**: IMMEDIATELY an info slide that defuses the resulting objection. "It takes less than 60 seconds a day."

### Past attempts

- **Purpose**: prospect admits previous solutions failed. Your product no longer needs to overcome that objection — it's already dead.
- **Sample**: "What have you tried before to solve this?"
- **Options**: include the common failed solutions in your category. Let them self-select into "tried and failed."

## Identity / escalation (negative) questions

### Past attempt emotion

- **Purpose**: shame admission. Identity-level ownership.
- **Format**: single-select
- **Sample**: "When I think about past attempts, I feel..."
- **Options**: frustrated / embarrassed / ashamed / defeated / [positive: confident]

### Embarrassment trigger

- **Purpose**: activates visibility shame
- **Sample**: "Have you felt embarrassed about [showing/doing X] in public?"
- **Format**: yes/no

### Protective instinct

- **Purpose**: activates family/impact concerns
- **Sample**: "Do you worry about [this] affecting your [family/partner/kids]?"
- **Format**: yes/no

### Fear anchor

- **Purpose**: future-pacing cost of inaction
- **Sample**: "The #1 thing I'm afraid of is..."
- **Options**: should align with the darker outcomes your product prevents.

### Cost expansion

- **Purpose**: move pain from feeling to tangible consequence
- **Sample**: "How does this affect your career/relationships/finances?"
- **Format**: single or multi-select

## Agree-scale statements (Liven/Inflow style)

- **Purpose**: force self-diagnosis through repeated "yes, that's me" agreements. Works especially well for identity/mental-health products.
- **Format**: statement + 5-point scale (Strongly disagree → Strongly agree)
- **Samples**:
  - "I find myself stuck on the same thoughts for hours."
  - "I imagine every possible scenario that could go wrong."
  - "I often feel judged for being different."

Stack 10+ of these in a row. By the 10th "Strongly agree," the user has self-labelled.

---

## Solution-awareness / upsell questions

### Usage quantity (AOV anchor)

- **Purpose**: user self-prescribes the quantity. Bundle pricing on results page then feels justified, not pitched.
- **Sample**: "How many servings per day would you want?" or "How much protein per serving?"
- **Options**: must be available in your SKU lineup (single-bottle → 3-pack → 6-pack).

### Preference / flavor / format

- **Purpose**: increases personalization, increases anticipation, user imagines using the product
- **Sample**: "Which flavor would you prefer?" or "How do you prefer consuming this?"
- **Bonus**: can **directional-route** to different bundle offers per choice.

## "Shameful No" technique

Pre-close yes/no questions where the NO option is framed as an identity nobody wants to claim. Three in a row, right before the offer.

Example (Javvy):

1. "Do you want a better daily coffee?"
   - Yes
   - No, I love my unhealthy habits.
2. "Do you want to save money on coffee?"
   - Yes
   - No, I love overpaying.
3. "Do you want to skip coffee-shop lines?"
   - Yes
   - No, I love wasting time in lines.

Pre-closes three objections (taste, price, convenience) by making "no" feel ridiculous even to themselves. Three yeses in a row → offer drops → conversion.

## Commitment gate questions

Placed immediately before the offer. Two yes/no questions.

1. "Are you ready to finally [achieve outcome]?"
2. "Can you commit to [specific action, e.g. taking one capsule daily]?"

Saying "no" to the offer page after clicking yes here becomes psychologically inconsistent. 20-30% CVR lift measured on multi-brand tests.

---

## Attribution questions (late, optional)

After the user is invested, ask:

- "Where did you hear about us most recently?"
- "When was the last time you heard about us?"

These don't affect the recommendation — they give you marketing data. Place them LATE (after the emotional journey) so they don't distract.

---

## Final email capture

- **Not sold as "give us your email"** — framed as "where should we send your personalized results?"
- **Endowed-progress effect**: progress bar shows near completion. Users more willing to share contact info to see results they've invested 3+ minutes earning.
- **Mystery bonus**: offering a free gift for SMS opt-in increases SMS capture rate.
