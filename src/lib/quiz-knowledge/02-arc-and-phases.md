# Arc Frameworks — How to Sequence Questions

Three proven sequencing frameworks. Pick the one that matches your audience awareness and product type.

---

## Framework 1: The Gradualization Sequence (7 steps)

Sultanic / Genesis framework. Works for cold or problem-aware traffic on physical-condition products (supplements, pain, appearance).

1. **Isolate Symptoms** — fragment vague awareness into specific admissions ("Which of these happen to you weekly?" multi-select)
2. **Paint the Cost** — translate symptoms into lived consequences ("How do these affect you day to day?")
3. **Contrast Ideal vs Current** — create the gap ("Which best describes how you want to feel most days?")
4. **Highlight a Pattern** — insight panel with no question ("When sleep issues, wired-tired energy, and mental overactivity appear together, it usually points to a nervous system that never fully downshifts")
5. **Normalize and Validate** — insight panel ("Most people think this is just normal stress. It's not. It's common but fixable once you know what's happening")
6. **Future Contrast** — cost of inaction ("If nothing changes, which feels most likely over the next few years?")
7. **Self-reached Conclusion** — they've now decided buying is the logical next step

Sequence can repeat multiple times within a long quiz. Steps 1-3 are questions, 4-5 are interstitials, 6 is a question, 7 is implicit at the offer.

---

## Framework 2: The Transformational Arc (positive → neutral → negative)

Alen Sultanic. Works across every niche. The key insight: quiz funnels don't just identify customers, they **create** them by moving prospects from an information-seeking state through a problem state to a solution state.

### Phase 1: Positive questions (first third)

Use "towards" language. Aspirational. Make them feel GOOD before shifting.

- "I want to..."
- "I'd like to..."
- "I dream of..."
- "My dream body is..."

Multi-select lets them stack reasons to buy.

### Phase 2: Neutral questions (middle third)

Observations about current behavior. Not accusatory.

- "I tend to..."
- "Sometimes I..."
- "When stressed, I tend to..."

Slowly makes it personal without triggering defense.

### Phase 3: Negative questions (final third)

"I AM" language. Identity-level admissions.

- "When I think about past attempts, I feel..."
- "I've tried everything but still struggle with..."

Identity ownership is the magic. Once the prospect admits they have the problem, they must by default admit they need help.

### Power move: future-pacing fear

One question confronting inaction:

- "The #1 thing I'm afraid of is..."

### Pressure-release (optional)

After the negative section:

- "What would you like instead?"

Frames that they have CHOICE. Once they feel choice, they're more apt to act.

### Sample structure using this arc

1. [POSITIVE] "My dream outcome is..."
2. [POSITIVE] "I'm ready to invest because..."
3. [NEUTRAL] "On a typical day, my routine includes..."
4. [NEUTRAL] "When stressed, I tend to..."
5. [NEGATIVE] "When I think about past attempts, I feel..."
6. [NEGATIVE] "The #1 thing I'm afraid of is..."
7. [NEGATIVE] "I've tried everything but still struggle with..."
8. [PRESSURE-RELEASE] "To solve this, I'm looking for..."

## Framework 3: The 5-Phase Funnel (Lunavia model)

Works for 20-30 question quizzes on physical-condition products with a hard offer at the end.

### Phase 1: Entry & Segmentation (slides 1-3)

- Gender
- Age
- Goal (first aspirational question)

Gender and age are zero-cognitive-load. The goal question immediately follows and tells the quiz what to mirror on the results page.

Place a **social proof slide** right after the goal question. "100,000+ people have successfully X." Bandwagon effect activated before the hard questions begin.

### Phase 2: Problem Diagnosis (slides 5-13)

Confront the condition. Where. How many. How severe. How long. What symptoms. Multi-select symptom question is the conversion weapon here — every checked box is a micro-confession.

Quantify the problem: a vague "I have X" becomes "I have moderate severity on 3-5 X with Y, Z, and A symptoms."

### Phase 3: Behavioral Assessment (slides 14-18)

Habits. Routine frequency. Lifestyle. Then IMMEDIATELY an info slide that defuses the resulting objection. Example: "It takes less than 60 seconds a day." Objection destroyed before it formed.

### Phase 4: Emotional Escalation (slides 19-26)

This is where most quiz builders go soft. Don't.

- Embarrassment: "Have you felt embarrassed about showing X in public?"
- Protective instinct: "Do you worry about Y affecting your family?"
- Motivation commitment: "How motivated are you to INVEST in finally solving this?" (note: INVEST, not SPEND)
- Time pressure via real events: "Do you have an important event coming up?"

Embarrassment hits shame. Protection hits family. Real events hit personal urgency.

Competitive destruction belongs in this phase:
- *"Why Most Treatments Fail"* — insight slide
- *"Most X stay on the surface. Our Y penetrates to the root."* — new mechanism positioning
- One slide, every competitor destroyed

### Phase 5: Commitment Gate (slides 27-28)

Two yes/no questions before the offer:

1. "Are you ready to finally [outcome]?"
2. "Can you commit to [specific action] daily?"

Saying "no" to the offer after clicking yes here is psychologically inconsistent. Can boost CVR 20-30%.

## Framework 4: The 5-Stage Psych Funnel (Liven model)

Works best for "emotional / identity" products: mental health, personality, overthinking, ADHD-related, relationships, behavioral apps.

1. **Self-Label** (Q1-7) — zero-friction questions about behaviour (frequency, age, gender). Plants the label by having the user affirm it about themselves.
2. **Pain Deepen** (Q8-17) — **agree-scale statements** (not questions). "I find myself stuck on the same thoughts." 10+ affirmations force deep investment.
3. **Cost Expand** (Q18-19) — move pain from feelings to tangible cost (career, money, relationships).
4. **Hope Reframe** — interstitial that reframes the flaw as a superpower if channeled right. "87% of X are more empathetic and intelligent." Perfectly timed after cost expansion.
5. **Future Cast / Pseudo-Diagnosis** — result screen with clinical visuals (score, type, color-coded severity). Not a quiz result — a **diagnosis**. Prescription loads next.

Key techniques in this framework:

- **Agree-scale statements** instead of questions. "I often catastrophize" with an agree scale forces self-diagnosis more than "Do you catastrophize?"
- **Format rotation** is critical for long quizzes (30+ questions). Switch between yes/no → multi-select → emoji → 1-5 scale → statement agreement to prevent fatigue.
- **Mascot or voice** dropping in after heavy answers with reassurance. Breaks clinical coldness. Turns a form into a conversation.
- **Education drops** mid-quiz introduce clinical concepts (e.g. "Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria") that reframe shame as neurology. Reader thinks "there's a name for what I feel" and massive trust is built.

---

## Picking a framework

| Audience | Product | Framework |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| Cold, problem-aware | Physical (supplements, pain, appearance) | Gradualization Sequence + 5-Phase Funnel |
| Solution-aware, warm | Commoditized product | Transformational Arc (shorter) |
| Identity/lifestyle | Apps, mental health, beauty | Liven 5-Stage |

Mix freely. Arc + Gradualization stacks especially well in 20-question funnels.
